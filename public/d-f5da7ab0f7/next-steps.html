ScaleWithMe — Next Steps

Bottom line: We need to set ScaleWithMe's direction together — who it's for, how we make money, the funnel, which workflows come first and who owns each, and our first public proof. To pressure-test one way of building it, I took a single slice — turn each stage of starting a business into a real tool + a live build — and ran stage one (defining the customer) end to end into a usable personas deliverable and two working tools (linked below). Treat that as a proof of concept, not a starting point — one example, not the plan. Give it a read, react, and send me your thoughts.

1 · The approach I tested

  • The lens: treat starting a business as a series of stages / workflows we can each turn into a real tool + a live build. "Business through technology," made concrete.
  • I went deep on stage one — persona / best-fit-customer development — as a proof of concept: can we take one stage and turn it into a genuinely usable workflow and tool?
  • What came out: our customer personas (a real, useful input for us), an installable tool, and a live web app — all linked below.
  • The use case — top of funnel to a DFY customer, one journey: a non-technical founder (our Vision-First Founder) sees a live-build clip on social → runs the free Avatar Builder web app, answers 5 questions, and gets three ranked personas she can actually use → drops her email to keep them → nurture pulls her to the weekly live-build, where she watches us take a persona from the tool and turn it into a landing page live on the $0 stack → she joins the membership for the full toolkit + weekly builds → she hits the wall (she wants it built, not to build it herself) → converts to DFY, and we build it for her. Free artifact at the top, a paying custom-build customer at the bottom.
  • Two tools, one for each of our first two personas: the plugin (install in Claude Code) fits the technical Stuck Shipper; the no-install web app fits the non-technical Vision-First Founder. One workflow, two front doors — each meets a different avatar where they already are.
  • The honest part: this is step one of a long road. Naming, domain, positioning, pricing, launch, delivery, support — lots of workflows still to solve. That's the opportunity, and it's ours to build together.
  • Closing the funnel gap: the web tool captures email at the point of use, and the plugin points its users to the same signup through a closing CTA — a free tool 500 people use should be 500 emails on the list. That's just the plumbing. The nurture sequence — which email tool, the comment→DM mechanic, the path to the webinar — is Jens's wheelhouse.

Tell me where you land, push back, add what's missing.

    Does this hold as our model — and where does DFY (custom builds) fit alongside it?

  • Which stages/workflows next — and who's drawn to which? (self-select by interest and strength, not top-down — see the menu below)

And what's the nurture engine — email tool, sequence, the comment→DM mechanic? Jens runs a proven comment → DM → email engine; his wheelhouse to lead.

  • Vision & curriculum — Jens and I share it. The northstar — "business through technology" — is Jake's line; for carrying vision + curriculum forward day to day, I propose Jens and I hold it jointly. Agree?

Appendix — a starting menu of workflows we could automate

Not a roadmap. Raw material for the "which workflows next — and who's drawn to which" question: a menu to react to, cut, add to, and claim. Each is a real stage of starting/running a business → a live build + a tool candidate, the same way persona development became one.

Start & define

Build

agent teammates ("Claude Code as your [social manager / content strategist / copywriter]") · automation glue (n8n + Claude Code + Cloudflare) · the micro-SaaS factory rig (idea → scaffold → deploy → monetize) · content engine (carousels + vibe-edited video) · skill / plugin builder · second-brain / knowledge ops · landing page / site generation · the MVP itself · brand kit · AI-built-app security review

Sell

positioning one-pager · messaging (feature → outcome) · pricing & packaging · hook / headline / ad-creative generator · channel finder · content repurposing engine · lead capture + funnel wiring · cold outreach / email sequences

Serve

customer interview kit · quote / estimate generator · proposal generator · onboarding flow · support / FAQ assistant · analytics & reporting dashboard · invoicing / admin automation

Ops & legal

formation / state-picker checklist · bookkeeping categorization

The point isn't to build all of these — each is a candidate for "a real tool + a live build," and we each pull the ones we're drawn to.
