Why the AI-Displaced Professional is the beachhead

She has the highest pain and urgency of any segment (existential loss + a severance clock), sits in the largest and fastest-growing market (AI-cited layoffs are ~56% of all layoffs and climbing), is highly reachable (LinkedIn "open to work" + r/layoffs are concentrated, high-intent), and generates the most viral proof (a laid-off person building real income out-shares any revenue screenshot). The hook is our most differentiated and on-brand — the tool that replaced you is the leverage to build your own thing — and it's the biggest underserved gap: the whole support ecosystem retrains people for another job; nobody helps them build their own income.

The risk to manage (not a reason to demote) — ability-to-pay / runway and high-touch delivery. Solve it with a results-first ladder (free assessment → low-risk trial → paid 90-day program + community) and rigorous proof-over-promise. Severance + urgency means they will invest in a credible path; bootcamps prove displaced workers pay for transformation.

The other three are designed-in, served differently:

  • Stuck Shipper (#2) — best pure economics and most self-serve; our build-in-public proof engine. The technical laid-off worker folds in here.
  • Vision-First Founder (#3) — budget + can't DIY → guided build path, with DFY as the escape hatch.
  • AI-Curious Owner (#4) — biggest but most diffuse; buys outcomes → à-la-carte tools, one-time → subscription.

One funnel does not fit all four. The Professional feeds the assessment → 90-day program; the Shipper feeds the membership; the Owner feeds the tool/SMB line; the Founder can go either way. Validate willingness-to-pay for each before committing.
