The three, in brief

① Stuck ShipperPrimary
The technical builder who can ship a product but can't get customers. He treats selling as an afterthought and has no best-fit-customer foundation to aim his tactics at. We lead here — cheapest to reach, best proof-spreader, most on-brand. → membership.

② Vision-First FounderSecondary
The non-technical operator with a validated idea and real budget who can't turn it into a product — hiring devs is expensive and slow, and no-code caps out. Converts readily; served via a guided build path, with DFY as the escape hatch for those who'd rather buy the outcome.

③ AI-Curious OwnerTertiary
The established SMB owner quietly losing time or money to one manual bottleneck, who knows AI could help but can't tell real from hype and has no time to figure it out. Buys outcomes, not a membership — reached per-vertical and sold à-la-carte tools (one-time → subscription).

Why the Stuck Shipper is the beachhead

He's the cheapest to reach (dense, networked communities), the best proof-spreader (build-in-public rewards real results — our exact format), and the most on-brand (Claude-native, near-free stack). Even with lower willingness to pay than the other two, his low CAC and fast flywheel make him the right audience beachhead. Lead all launch content and the membership offer here.

The other two are designed-in, served differently:

  • Vision-First Founder converts more readily (budget, can't DIY) — capture via a guided build path, with DFY as the escape hatch for those who'd rather buy the outcome.
  • AI-Curious Owner is the biggest but most diffuse market and buys outcomes, not membership — reach per-vertical and sell à-la-carte tools (the reskinnable quote/estimate generator), one-time → subscription.

One funnel does not fit all three. The Stuck Shipper feeds the membership; the Owner feeds the tool/SMB line; the Founder can go either way. Validate willingness-to-pay for each before committing the offer.
